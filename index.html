Selected Research

Ongoing and recent work

The following summaries describe the main ideas of my recent research projects. Since several manuscripts are still under submission, I present the work by topic rather than by paper title.

Second-author project Jan 2026 – Mar 2026

Selective safety unlearning for multimodal large language models

This project studies how to reduce harmful multimodal behavior without causing blanket refusal on benign inputs. The core idea is to intervene at the multimodal connector, estimate sample-specific risk from explicit and implicit evidence, and suppress only the low-rank risk-aligned directions while preserving utility-relevant components.

  • Worked on experiment design, cloud-based training, figure production, and empirical analysis.
  • Focused on cross-modal risk, over-refusal, and out-of-distribution generalization in multimodal safety settings.
First-author project Jan 2026 – Mar 2026

Refusal calibration under benign multi-turn dialogue history

This project investigates a subtle failure mode in aligned language models: safe requests may be rejected after benign but compliance-heavy conversational history. I built experiments around history-induced refusal drift and explored a lightweight calibration strategy that separates the original refusal boundary from history-driven conservative bias.

  • Implemented the main experimental pipeline in Python and completed end-to-end analysis and writing.
  • Emphasized stability across conversation histories rather than simply making the model more permissive.
Third-author project Nov 2025 – Jan 2026

Budget-efficient red teaming for LLM safety evaluation

This work reframes red teaming as a budget-constrained failure discovery problem. The system combines cognitive profiling, tree search, and iterative strategy refinement to discover diverse policy violations earlier and with fewer queries.

  • Used Python and API-based evaluation across four large models.
  • Helped coordinate experiments, data analysis, and visualization.
Fourth-author project Dec 2025 – Mar 2026

Remote sensing vision-language pre-training with hierarchical semantics

This project explores how to bridge global scene understanding and local fine-grained semantics in remote sensing imagery. The framework uses multi-granularity supervision, regional semantic mining, and lightweight visual adaptation to improve remote sensing vision-language representation learning.

  • Participated in experimental iteration and figure design.
  • Contributed to project writing and visual presentation of results.
